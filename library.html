The Digital Repository

Library & Resources

A scholarly reading list for students of the Martinist tradition. Texts are organized by historical category, from the primary founders through direct influences and the 19th-century revival. Free digital editions are linked where verified copies exist at the Internet Archive or Project Gutenberg. Manuscript sources are noted with their archival locations.

All titles listed are historically attested. No works have been invented or speculatively attributed. Original-language titles are given where applicable, with English translations noted when available.

I
Primary Martinist Sources Pasqually · Saint-Martin · Willermoz
Martines de Pasqually c. 1727–1774

Founder of the Ordre des Élus Coëns. His doctrine was transmitted primarily through manuscripts and ritual instructions rather than published works.

  • Traité de la Réintégration des êtres dans leurs premières propriétés, vertus et puissances spirituelles et divines (c. 1770; first published 1899, ed. Papus) The foundational theurgical and cosmological text of the Élus Coëns. No complete English translation in print. MS · BML, Fonds Willermoz MS 5920–5930
  • Catéchismes des Élus Coëns & Instructions aux Réaux-Croix Ritual catechisms and instructions to the highest-degree initiates. Manuscript only. MS · Bibliothèque municipale de Lyon
  • Correspondence with Willermoz and Saint-Martin (c. 1767–1772) Primary epistolary source for Pasquallian doctrine. MS · BML, Fonds Willermoz
Louis-Claude de Saint-Martin 1743–1803

Published as Le Philosophe Inconnu (The Unknown Philosopher). The most prolific writer of the Martinist current, moving from Pasquallian theurgy toward Böhmian interior mysticism.

  • Des Erreurs et de la Vérité (1775) His first major work; a theosophical critique of rationalism. English: Of Errors and of Truth, trans. E.B. Penny (1884). Read Free
  • Tableau naturel des rapports qui existent entre Dieu, l'Homme et l'Univers (1782) His cosmological and anthropological system. English: Natural Table of Correspondences, trans. E.B. Penny (1900).
  • L'Homme de Désir (1790) Devotional mysticism; his fullest turn toward interior Christianity. English: The Man of Desire, trans. A.E. Waite (1910).
  • Le Nouvel Homme (1796) On the regeneration of the inner man. English: The New Man, trans. E.B. Penny (1888).
  • Le Crocodile, ou la Guerre du Bien et du Mal (1799) An esoteric prose epic on spiritual warfare; his most literary work.
  • De l'Esprit des Choses (1800, 2 vols.) His mature philosophical synthesis.
  • Le Ministère de l'Homme-Esprit (1802) His final and most developed theosophical work. English: Man: His True Nature and Ministry, trans. E.B. Penny. Read Free
  • Correspondance inédite… et Kirchberger, baron de Liebistorf (ed. Schauer & Chuquet, Paris 1862) Central to understanding his Böhmian development; essential primary source.
Jean-Baptiste Willermoz 1730–1824

Silk merchant of Lyon and primary organizer of initiatic Martinism. Integrated Pasquallian doctrine into the Rectified Scottish Rite.

  • Instructions Secrètes aux Chevaliers Bienfaisants de la Cité Sainte Inner doctrinal instructions of the Rectified Scottish Rite. MS · BML, Fonds Willermoz MS 5920–5930
  • Instruction aux Grands Profès Instructions to the highest initiates of the RER; synthesizes Pasquallian doctrine with Masonic structure. MS · BML, Fonds Willermoz
  • Correspondence with Pasqually, Saint-Martin, and Prince Charles of Hesse-Kassel The principal archival source for the history of the Élus Coëns and Rectified Scottish Rite. MS · BML, Fonds Willermoz MS 5920–5930
II
Direct Influences Böhme · Swedenborg · Earlier Mystics & Theosophers
Jakob Böhme 1575–1624

German Lutheran mystic and theosopher. After c. 1788, Saint-Martin translated Böhme's major works into French; Böhme is the single largest influence on his later writings.

  • Aurora, oder Morgenröthe im Aufgang (1612) English: The Aurora, trans. John Sparrow (1656; repr. 1914). Read Free
  • Signatura Rerum (1621) English: The Signature of All Things, trans. John Ellistone (1651; repr. 1912). Read Free
  • Mysterium Magnum (1623) His great commentary on Genesis. Trans. John Sparrow (1654; repr. 1924, 2 vols.). Part I Part II
  • The Works of Jacob Behmen (ed. William Law, London 1764–1781, 4 vols.) The standard collected English edition. Vol. I Vol. II
Emanuel Swedenborg 1688–1772

Swedish scientist and mystic. His doctrines of correspondences and spiritual cosmology were closely studied in Willermoz's circle; Dom Pernety translated him into French for that milieu.

  • Arcana Caelestia (London, 1749–1756, 8 vols.) Encyclopedic exegesis of Genesis; foundational statement of the doctrine of correspondences. Read Free
  • De Caelo et Ejus Mirabilibus et de Inferno (1758) English: Heaven and Hell. The most widely read of his theological works. Project Gutenberg Archive
  • De Nova Hierosolyma et Ejus Doctrina Caelesti (1758) English: The New Jerusalem and Its Heavenly Doctrine.
  • Vera Christiana Religio (Amsterdam, 1771) English: The True Christian Religion.
Earlier Christian Mystics & Theosophers
  • Theologia Germanica (anonymous, 14th c.) Published by Luther (1516, 1518). Deeply influential on Böhme and in turn on Saint-Martin.
  • De Occulta Philosophia Libri Tres — H.C. Agrippa von Nettesheim (1531) Encyclopedic occult philosophy; influenced the Élus Coëns ritual tradition.
  • Theologia Mystica — John Pordage (1683) Direct Böhmian theosophy; influential in the German tradition Saint-Martin engaged.
  • A Fountain of Gardens — Jane Leade (1696–1701, 4 vols.) Theosophical spiritual journal; Philadelphian Society writings circulated in Böhmian and Martinist circles.
  • Theosophia Practica — Johann Georg Gichtel (Leiden, 1722) Böhmian theosophy applied to spiritual practice; widely influential across European pietist and esoteric circles.
  • Theologia ex Idea Vitae Deducta — Friedrich Christoph Oetinger (1765) Major conduit of Böhmian ideas contemporaneous with Saint-Martin; combined Böhme with Lutheran theology and Kabbalah.
III
Revival & Later Developments Papus · Guaita · Péladan · Chaboseau · Waite
Gérard Encausse "Papus" 1865–1916

Primary organizer of the revived Martinist Order (1887); his publications systematized and popularized the tradition for a new generation of readers.

  • Le Tarot des Bohémiens (Paris, 1889) English: The Tarot of the Bohemians, trans. A.P. Morton (1892). Read Free
  • Martines de Pasqually: sa vie, ses pratiques magiques, son œuvre, ses disciples (Paris: Chamuel, 1895) The primary historical account of Pasqually's life and order.
  • Le Martinisme: histoire, doctrine et rites (Paris: Chamuel, 1899) The foundational account of Martinism as understood by the revived Order.
  • Louis-Claude de Saint-Martin: le philosophe inconnu (Paris: Chamuel, 1902)
  • La Kabbale: tradition secrète de l'Occident (Paris, 1892) English: The Qabalah: Secret Tradition of the West, trans. A. Morton (Weiser, 1977).
  • Traité de la Réintégration des êtres — by Martines de Pasqually; first published edition, ed. Papus (Paris: Chacornac, 1899)
Stanislas de Guaita 1861–1897

Poet and occultist; co-founder of the Ordre Kabbalistique de la Rose-Croix (1888); close associate of Papus and one of the most rigorous occult scholars of the French revival.

  • Essais de Sciences Maudites Three-volume magnum opus:
     · Vol. I: Au Seuil du Mystère (1886)
     · Vol. II: Le Temple de Satan (1891)
     · Vol. III: La Clef de la Magie Noire (1897)
Joséphin Péladan 1858–1918

Catholic esotericist; founder of the Ordre de la Rose-Croix du Temple et du Graal; associated with the Martinist milieu before his break with Guaita and Papus.

  • Comment on devient Mage (Paris: Chamuel, 1892)
  • Comment on devient Fée (Paris: Chamuel, 1893)
  • L'Art Idéaliste et Mystique (Paris: Chamuel, 1894) His aesthetic and spiritual manifesto.
Augustin Chaboseau 1868–1946

Co-founder with Papus of the revived Martinist Order; claimed an initiatic lineage descending through Henri Delaage.

  • Les Origines de la Franc-Maçonnerie: Le cycle celtique (Paris, 1912)
  • Une initiation au Moyen Âge (Paris, 1941) On initiatic transmission in medieval Christianity.
  • Extensive contributions to L'Initiation, the Martinist journal edited by Papus (1888–1914).
Arthur Edward Waite 1857–1942

British occultist and historian; primary transmitter of Martinist ideas into English; translator of Saint-Martin and Lévi.

  • The Unknown Philosopher: The Life of Louis Claude de Saint-Martin (London: Theosophical Publishing Society, 1901) The primary English-language study of Saint-Martin. Read Free
  • Saint-Martin the French Mystic and the Story of Modern Martinism (London: William Rider, 1922) Read Free
  • The Man of Desire — Saint-Martin, trans. Waite (London: Philip Wellby, 1910)
  • The Brotherhood of the Rosy Cross (London: William Rider, 1924) History of Rosicrucianism including its relationship to Martinist currents.
  • The Secret Tradition in Freemasonry (London: Rebman, 1911, 2 vols.)
  • The Holy Kabbalah (London: Williams & Norgate, 1929)
IV
Key Precursors & Associated Figures Lévi · Pernety · Fournié · Eckartshausen · Baader
Eliphas Lévi 1810–1875

Born Alphonse-Louis Constant. The most influential French occultist of the 19th century; direct precursor to Papus and the Martinist revival.

  • Dogme et Rituel de la Haute Magie (Paris, 1854–1856, 2 vols.) English: Transcendental Magic, trans. A.E. Waite (1896). Read Free
  • Histoire de la Magie (Paris, 1860) English: The History of Magic, trans. A.E. Waite (1913). Project Gutenberg
  • La Clef des Grands Mystères (Paris, 1861) English: The Key of the Mysteries, trans. A.E. Waite (Rider, 1959).
Dom Antoine-Joseph Pernety 1716–1796

Benedictine monk, alchemist, and theosopher; founded the Illuminés d'Avignon; translated Swedenborg into French for the Willermoz circle.

  • Dictionnaire Mytho-Hermétique (Paris, 1758) Standard alchemical reference work in European occult circles.
  • Les Fables Égyptiennes et Grecques dévoilées (Paris, 1758, 2 vols.)
  • Du Ciel et de ses Merveilles — Swedenborg, trans. Pernety (Berlin, 1782) The version of Heaven and Hell circulating in Willermoz's milieu.
Abbé Pierre Fournié c. 1738–c. 1803

Priest and Élus Coëns initiate; author of one of the clearest published expositions of Pasquallian doctrine.

  • Ce que nous avons été, ce que nous sommes, et ce que nous deviendrons (London, 1801) An important published document presenting Pasquallian cosmological doctrine.
Karl von Eckartshausen 1752–1803

Bavarian theosopher; one of the most widely read Christian esoteric authors of the early 19th century.

  • Die Wolke über dem Heiligtum (Munich, 1802) English: The Cloud upon the Sanctuary, trans. Isabel de Steiger, preface by A.E. Waite (1903).
  • Aufschlüsse zur Magie aus geprüften Erfahrungen (Munich, 1788–1792, 4 vols.)
Franz von Baader 1765–1841

German Catholic theosopher; the most significant philosopher to synthesize Böhme with academic philosophy; widely read in Martinist circles.

  • Fermenta Cognitionis (Berlin, 1822–1825, 6 pts.) Aphoristic theosophical philosophy.
  • Vorlesungen über J. Böhme's Theologoumena und Philosopheme (1835–1836)
  • Sämtliche Werke, ed. Hoffmann et al. (Leipzig, 1851–1860, 16 vols.)
V
Principal Scholarly Reference Works For Students & Researchers
  • The Unknown Philosopher — A.E. Waite (London: Theosophical Publishing Society, 1901) Primary English-language study of Saint-Martin. Read Free
  • Saint-Martin, le Philosophe Inconnu: sa vie et ses écrits — Jacques Matter (Paris: Didier, 1862) The earliest major biographical and doctrinal study of Saint-Martin.
  • Un Mystique Lyonnais: Jean-Baptiste Willermoz (1730–1824) — Alice Joly (Mâcon: Protat Frères, 1938) The primary monograph on Willermoz; indispensable for the Rectified Scottish Rite.
  • La Franc-Maçonnerie Templière et Occultiste aux XVIIIe et XIXe Siècles — René Le Forestier (Paris: Aubier-Montaigne, 1970) Major scholarly history of the Rectified Scottish Rite and Élus Coëns; the standard academic reference.
  • Les Sources Occultes du Romantisme: Illuminisme — Théosophie, 1770–1820 — Auguste Viatte (Paris: Champion, 1928, 2 vols.) Standard study of theosophical literature in France; essential context for Saint-Martin.
  • Louis-Claude de Saint-Martin et le Martinisme — Robert Amadou (Paris: Griffon d'Or, 1946) The foremost 20th-century scholarly account of the tradition.
  • Access to Western Esotericism — Antoine Faivre (Albany: SUNY Press, 1994) Foundational academic framework for the study of Western esotericism.
  • Dictionary of Gnosis and Western Esotericism — ed. Wouter J. Hanegraaff (Leiden: Brill, 2005) Standard reference work; authoritative articles on all major Martinist figures and themes.
  • Les Sociétés Secrètes de Paris — Pierre Geyraud (Paris: Émile-Paul, 1940) Sociological survey of Parisian occult movements including the revived Martinist Order.
